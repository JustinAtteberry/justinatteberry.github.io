Movie Correlation Analysis

I used Python in a Jupyter Notebook to look at the correlation between the data in this dataset from Kaggle. Looking at the heatmap created with Seaborn, the strongest correlation is between budget and gross earnings. Meaning that as the budget for a film gets higher, there is a stronger chance that the gross earnings will be higher. One interesting thing from the heatmap is the correlation between score and runtime. There is a slight correlation (0.41) between the two and this would mean that as the film length gets longer, it is kind of more likely to have a higher score. I think this is interesting as a lot of more popular movies are starting to push the 3 hour mark on runtime and they are often well recieved even though they are longer to finish watching.
